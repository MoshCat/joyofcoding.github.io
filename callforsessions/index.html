Proposals (written in English) should contain the following elements

  • Session title
  • A one page summary - describing the topic of the session, its objectives and the required means of achieving those objectives
  • Abstract and bio - two 100-200 word abstracts, describing (1) the format of the session (2) a bio of (each of) the organizer(s), both suitable for publication on the conference Web site

Session attributes

  • Sessions can be in the form of a workshop, tutorial, talk, discussion, hands-on coding session or other form of 'un-session'
  • Encourage participation: we prefer sessions that enable attendees to actively participate, through hands-on coding, discussion or other inspiring ways of interaction
  • Introduce novelty: topics should pertain to new and emerging technologies, introducing new and improved ways of dealing with existing challenges or dealing with entirely new challenges altogether
  • Be technology-agnostic: a workshop preferably imposes little or no barriers to entry for potential participants, i.e. participants will not be required to have operating system 'A' installed, or own a (commercial license) for software product 'B', or be proficient in programming language 'C' in order to be able to participate.
  • Time-boxed execution: the session length will be either 60 or 120 minutes. We like to keep a tight schedule, so your degree of freedom lies in the number of features or stories you will deliver.

Selection criteria

All submissions will be evaluated and selected based on the above mentioned session requirements and the relation of the proposal to the overall conference theme and programme. This call closes on the 1st of January 2013.
